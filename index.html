APPROACH
Our message was created to be expressed to the neutral audience in various social classes. We used the power of emotional and logical appeal to bring awareness to the issue. In order to bring the neutral audience to our side of the spectrum, we catered our ads to appeal to a specific demographic. We kept in mind one of the most important reasons that the regulations exist, which is to keep Canadians from going bankrupt. We used ethos and logos to provide statistics on debt, income, housing prices, and the stress test.
RESEARCH
Our research centred in on the newest changes to the systems involved in home ownership, but to truly grasp the effects it would have on buyers we branched out into the topic of debt itself to see the effects that a lack of these policies may possibly create. We focused on how these policies protect the consumer and the family, on an individual level.

In our research, we found that many new to or outside those who have already bought a home are unsure about mortgage or do completely understand just how serious a factor it is to home ownership.
ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS
Some find the discussion of private funds to be a personal thing, and discussion of someone's financial status could be taken in the wrong context or considered rude. A very important consideration is to not come across as saying one should own home but instead that if one decides to own a home, then consider our argument.
